# CDK2 Three-Receptor Complementarity Record

## Input

The matrix contains 60 shared ligands and three prepared receptor
conformers. Each cell is the rank-1 Vina docking score, where a more negative
score is better.

- `CDK2_1AQ1_A_prepared`
- `CDK2_1HCL_A_aligned_prepared`
- `CDK2_1JVP_P_aligned_prepared`

All 180 receptor-ligand pairs have successful scores. The matrix and summary
are generated by `scripts/build_score_matrix.py`.

## Score Correlation

| Receptor pair | Spearman | Pearson |
|---|---:|---:|
| 1AQ1 - 1HCL | 0.789 | 0.810 |
| 1AQ1 - 1JVP | 0.802 | 0.838 |
| 1HCL - 1JVP | 0.710 | 0.760 |

The lower HCL-JVP correlation indicates more score variation between these
two structures, but correlation alone does not prove useful biological
complementarity.

## Top10 Complementarity

| Receptor pair | Top10 overlap | Jaccard | Shared Top10 actives |
|---|---:|---:|---:|
| 1AQ1 - 1HCL | 7/10 | 0.538 | 3 |
| 1AQ1 - 1JVP | 7/10 | 0.538 | 3 |
| 1HCL - 1JVP | 9/10 | 0.818 | 3 |

The union of all three Top10 lists contains only four active ligands:
`CDK2_A0001`, `CDK2_A0003`, `CDK2_A0009`, and `CDK2_A0010`. All four are
already present in the 1AQ1 Top10 list. Thus, within this 60-ligand
benchmark, HCL and JVP add no new early active coverage beyond 1AQ1.

## Simple Ensemble Baselines

| Method | ROC-AUC | PR-AUC | BEDROC | EF1% | EF5% | EF10% |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| 1AQ1 | 0.644 | 0.459 | 0.653 | 6 | 4 | 3 |
| 1HCL | 0.576 | 0.285 | 0.377 | 0 | 4 | 2 |
| 1JVP | 0.718 | 0.436 | 0.531 | 6 | 4 | 2 |
| Three-receptor min score | 0.704 | 0.471 | 0.623 | 6 | 4 | 3 |
| Three-receptor mean score | 0.690 | 0.424 | 0.542 | 6 | 4 | 2 |

The minimum-score ensemble does not add Top10 active coverage in this
benchmark. The mean-score ensemble is more conservative, but its early
metrics are not uniformly better.

## Interpretation For QUBO

This result does not justify selecting all three receptors or selecting the
single receptor with the best ROC-AUC. Instead, it provides the required
structured input for subset selection:

1. Individual receptor quality differs by metric.
2. HCL and JVP are highly redundant in their Top10 ligand sets.
3. Score variation exists even when early active coverage does not improve.
4. A future QUBO objective should penalize redundancy and receptor count,
   while rewarding validated active coverage or ligand-level complementarity.

The conclusion is limited by the 10-active/50-decoy DUD-E subset and by the
same benchmark being used for comparison. It is not an independent test of
generalization.
