# QUBO And Classical Subset Comparison

## Evaluation Protocol

The coverage-aware QUBO selected `1AQ1 + 1JVP` using train data only. The
same subset and aggregation rule were then evaluated on train, validation,
and test. The comparison table also includes each single receptor and all
three receptors.

## Minimum-Score Aggregation

| Split | 1AQ1 ROC-AUC | 1JVP ROC-AUC | QUBO subset ROC-AUC | All three ROC-AUC |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| train | 0.694 | 0.700 | 0.722 | 0.722 |
| validation | 0.200 | 0.550 | 0.450 | 0.450 |
| test | 0.800 | 0.850 | 0.850 | 0.850 |

The QUBO subset improves over either individual receptor on train, but not
on the small validation/test sets. It is equal to the all-receptor minimum
score because 1HCL does not change the minimum for these ligand rows.

## Interpretation

This is an important negative result: constructing a QUBO does not guarantee
an improvement in screening enrichment. The current QUBO successfully
selects a reproducible subset and respects the train-only selection rule, but
the held-out evidence does not show a benefit over 1JVP alone.

The result should not be overinterpreted because validation and test each
contain only two actives. A larger ligand benchmark and more diverse receptor
conformers are required before judging the subset objective.
